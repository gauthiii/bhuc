# BHUC Screening Scoring Rules

> Validated instrument scoring and risk-banding rules for the BHUC Risk Identification Agent.
> Core mental-health spine (PHQ-9, GAD-7, C-SSRS) plus the SUD screening battery
> (NIDA Quick Screen, AUDIT, DAST-10, Craving & Triggers, SOWS, BAM, SOCRATES).

## PHQ-9 — scoring and severity bands

<p><strong>PHQ-9 (Patient Health Questionnaire-9) — depression severity</strong></p><p>Nine items, each scored 0–3 over the last two weeks: 0 = Not at all, 1 = Several days, 2 = More than half the days, 3 = Nearly every day. Total range 0–27.</p><table border="1"><thead><tr><th>Total score</th><th>Severity</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td>0–4</td><td>Minimal / none</td></tr><tr><td>5–9</td><td>Mild</td></tr><tr><td>10–14</td><td>Moderate</td></tr><tr><td>15–19</td><td>Moderately severe</td></tr><tr><td>20–27</td><td>Severe</td></tr></tbody></table><p><strong>Item 9 (self-harm) safety flag</strong></p><p>PHQ-9 item 9 asks about thoughts of being better off dead or self-harm. Any non-zero response on item 9 is a positive self-harm screen and triggers the BHUC crisis pathway regardless of the total score.</p><p>Clinical action thresholds: total ≥10 suggests clinically significant depression warranting clinician review; ≥20 indicates severe depression.</p>


## GAD-7 — scoring and severity bands

<p><strong>GAD-7 (Generalized Anxiety Disorder-7) — anxiety severity</strong></p><p>Seven items, each scored 0–3 over the last two weeks (same 0–3 anchors as PHQ-9). Total range 0–21.</p><table border="1"><thead><tr><th>Total score</th><th>Severity</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td>0–4</td><td>Minimal</td></tr><tr><td>5–9</td><td>Mild</td></tr><tr><td>10–14</td><td>Moderate</td></tr><tr><td>15–21</td><td>Severe</td></tr></tbody></table><p>A cutoff of ≥10 is the recommended threshold for further evaluation of generalized anxiety disorder. GAD-7 also screens for panic, social anxiety, and PTSD when elevated.</p>


## C-SSRS — screening version, branching, and high-risk criteria

<p><strong>Columbia Suicide Severity Rating Scale (C-SSRS) — screener</strong></p><p>The C-SSRS screener assesses suicidal ideation and behavior. Ideation is asked first; behavior questions follow.</p><p><strong>Ideation (past month)</strong></p><ol><li>Wish to be dead</li><li>Non-specific active suicidal thoughts</li><li>Active ideation with any methods (no plan) without intent to act</li><li>Active ideation with some intent to act, without specific plan</li><li>Active ideation with specific plan and intent</li></ol><p><strong>Behavior (lifetime and past 3 months)</strong></p><p>Actual attempt, interrupted attempt, aborted attempt, preparatory acts, and non-suicidal self-injurious behavior.</p><p><strong>Branching / risk logic</strong></p><ul><li>A <strong>Yes</strong> on ideation item 1 or 2 reveals items 3–5.</li><li><strong>Yes</strong> on ideation items 4 or 5, or <strong>any</strong> suicidal behavior, is a positive high-risk screen → immediate escalation.</li><li>Any positive behavior in the past 3 months is high acuity.</li></ul>


## NIDA Quick Screen — SUD gateway screen and routing

<p><strong>NIDA Quick Screen V1.0 — substance-use gateway</strong></p><p>Four items asking "In the past year, how often have you used the following?" for (1) Alcohol — 5+ drinks/day for men, 4+ for women; (2) Tobacco products; (3) Prescription drugs for non-medical reasons; (4) Illegal drugs. Each answered on a 5-point frequency scale: Never (0), Once or twice (1), Monthly (2), Weekly (3), Daily or almost daily (4).</p><p><strong>This is a router, not a scored band.</strong> Any response above "Never" is a positive screen for that substance and unlocks the matching full instrument:</p><ul><li>Alcohol (item 1) &gt; 0 → administer <strong>AUDIT</strong>.</li><li>Prescription non-medical (item 3) or illegal drugs (item 4) &gt; 0 → administer <strong>DAST-10</strong>.</li><li>Tobacco (item 2) &gt; 0 → advise cessation support (no scored instrument).</li></ul><p>Flags emitted: <code>alcohol_use</code>, <code>tobacco_use</code>, <code>drug_use</code>. All NIDA responses are 42 CFR Part 2 (SUD) data → <code>part2_sud</code>.</p>


## AUDIT — alcohol use scoring and WHO risk zones

<p><strong>AUDIT (Alcohol Use Disorders Identification Test, WHO) — 10 items, total 0–40.</strong></p><p>Items 1–8 score 0–4; items 9–10 score 0, 2, or 4. Administered when the NIDA Quick Screen alcohol item is positive.</p><table border="1"><thead><tr><th>Total score</th><th>WHO risk zone</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td>0–7</td><td>Zone I — low risk</td></tr><tr><td>8–15</td><td>Zone II — hazardous use</td></tr><tr><td>16–19</td><td>Zone III — harmful use</td></tr><tr><td>20–40</td><td>Zone IV — possible dependence</td></tr></tbody></table><p><strong>Cutoff ≥ 8</strong> is a positive screen (flag <code>audit_positive</code>) and unlocks the Craving &amp; Triggers module, BAM, and SOCRATES. <strong>≥ 20</strong> (flag <code>possible_dependence</code>) indicates likely alcohol dependence warranting priority clinical assessment. SUD data → <code>part2_sud</code>.</p>


## DAST-10 — drug use scoring and severity

<p><strong>DAST-10 (Drug Abuse Screening Test) — 10 items, past 12 months, total 0–10.</strong></p><p>"Drug use" excludes alcohol. Each Yes = 1 except item 3 ("Are you always able to stop using drugs when you want to?"), which is reverse-scored (Yes = 0). Administered when the NIDA Quick Screen drug items are positive.</p><table border="1"><thead><tr><th>Total score</th><th>Degree of problem</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td>0</td><td>No problems reported</td></tr><tr><td>1–2</td><td>Low level</td></tr><tr><td>3–5</td><td>Moderate level</td></tr><tr><td>6–8</td><td>Substantial level</td></tr><tr><td>9–10</td><td>Severe level</td></tr></tbody></table><p><strong>Cutoff ≥ 3</strong> is a positive screen (flag <code>dast_positive</code>) and unlocks SOWS, the Craving &amp; Triggers module, BAM, and SOCRATES. <strong>≥ 9</strong> (flag <code>dast_severe</code>) is high acuity. SUD data → <code>part2_sud</code>.</p>


## Craving & Triggers — BHUC custom module

<p><strong>Craving &amp; Triggers — BHUC custom clinic module (NOT a validated or copyrighted instrument).</strong></p><p>Seven items, each 0–4, total 0–28. Captures craving frequency and intensity, ability to resist, trigger context (people/places, difficult emotions, social/celebratory situations), and after-use feelings. Administered when AUDIT ≥ 8. Operational bands only (no published cutoffs):</p><table border="1"><thead><tr><th>Total score</th><th>Craving level</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td>0–9</td><td>Low</td></tr><tr><td>10–19</td><td>Moderate</td></tr><tr><td>20–28</td><td>High (flag <code>high_craving</code>)</td></tr></tbody></table><p>High craving warrants clinician attention (relapse-risk / medication considerations) but is not, by itself, a suicide-risk escalation. SUD data → <code>part2_sud</code>. Because this is a non-validated clinic module, the agent must present its output as supportive context, not a diagnostic score.</p>


## SOWS — subjective opiate withdrawal scoring

<p><strong>SOWS (Subjective Opiate Withdrawal Scale, Handelsman et al. 1987) — 16 items, total 0–64.</strong></p><p>Public-domain self-report of current withdrawal symptoms (anxiety, yawning, perspiration, tearing, rhinorrhea, gooseflesh, tremor, hot/cold flushes, bone/muscle ache, restlessness, nausea, vomiting, muscle twitch, stomach cramps, and urge to use). Each rated 0–4: Not at all (0), A little (1), Moderately (2), Quite a bit (3), Extremely (4). Administered when DAST-10 ≥ 3.</p><table border="1"><thead><tr><th>Total score</th><th>Withdrawal severity</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td>1–10</td><td>Mild</td></tr><tr><td>11–20</td><td>Moderate</td></tr><tr><td>21–64</td><td>Severe (flag <code>severe_withdrawal</code>)</td></tr></tbody></table><p><strong>≥ 21 (severe withdrawal)</strong> is high acuity → urgent medical/clinician review for withdrawal management. SUD data → <code>part2_sud</code>.</p>


## BAM — Brief Addiction Monitor subscales

<p><strong>BAM (Brief Addiction Monitor, VA/Penn, Cacciola et al. 2013) — 17 items, past 30 days.</strong></p><p>A recovery-monitoring instrument (mixed day-count and 0–4 intensity anchors), administered when AUDIT ≥ 8 or DAST-10 ≥ 3. Scored as three subscales rather than a single total (canonical item numbers; item 7's 7A–7G drug-type elaboration is unscored):</p><table border="1"><thead><tr><th>Subscale</th><th>Items</th><th>Direction</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td>Use</td><td>4, 5, 6</td><td>Higher = more substance use</td></tr><tr><td>Risk factors</td><td>1, 2, 3, 8, 11, 15</td><td>Higher = more risk</td></tr><tr><td>Protective factors</td><td>9, 10, 12, 13, 14, 16</td><td>Higher = more protection</td></tr></tbody></table><p>Item 17 (satisfaction with recovery progress) is standalone and reverse-scored; it is in no subscale. There are no official cut-scores — BAM is interpreted ipsatively (against the patient's own prior scores). Item 8 (bothered by cravings/urges) scored 3–4 sets <code>high_craving</code> and warrants attention. SUD data → <code>part2_sud</code>.</p>


## SOCRATES — readiness-to-change subscales

<p><strong>SOCRATES v8 (Stages of Change Readiness and Treatment Eagerness Scale, Miller &amp; Tonigan) — 19 items, public domain.</strong></p><p>Self-report readiness measure administered when AUDIT ≥ 8 or DAST-10 ≥ 3; alcohol wording (8A) or drug wording (8D) is chosen by which path fired. Each item is a 1–5 Likert (1 = NO! Strongly disagree … 5 = YES! Strongly agree). Scored as three subscales (straight sums, no reverse items):</p><table border="1"><thead><tr><th>Subscale</th><th>Items</th><th>Range</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td>Recognition (Re)</td><td>1, 3, 7, 10, 12, 15, 17</td><td>7–35</td></tr><tr><td>Ambivalence (Am)</td><td>2, 6, 11, 16</td><td>4–20</td></tr><tr><td>Taking Steps (Ts)</td><td>4, 5, 8, 9, 13, 14, 18, 19</td><td>8–40</td></tr></tbody></table><p>Interpretation is by decile relative to a treatment-seeking population (low / medium / high per subscale). High Recognition and high Taking Steps are favorable; a low Ambivalence score is interpreted in relation to Recognition. SOCRATES informs treatment engagement and does not by itself set a suicide-risk band. SUD data → <code>part2_sud</code>.</p>


## BHUC risk banding and escalation rules

<p><strong>How instrument results map to a BHUC risk band</strong></p><p>Suicide risk (C-SSRS / PHQ-9 item 9) always governs the crisis pathway. The SUD battery contributes SUD acuity, which raises the band but does not replace suicide-risk logic.</p><table border="1"><thead><tr><th>Risk band</th><th>Criteria</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td>High</td><td>C-SSRS ideation item 4 or 5 positive; OR any C-SSRS suicidal behavior; OR PHQ-9 item 9 &gt; 0 with plan/intent; OR SOWS ≥ 21 (severe withdrawal); OR DAST-10 ≥ 9 (severe)</td></tr><tr><td>Moderate</td><td>PHQ-9 15–19 or GAD-7 15–21; OR C-SSRS ideation items 1–3 positive without intent/plan; OR AUDIT ≥ 16 (harmful/dependence); OR DAST-10 6–8 (substantial); OR high craving (Craving &amp; Triggers ≥ 20 or BAM item 8 = 3–4)</td></tr><tr><td>Low</td><td>PHQ-9 &lt; 15 and GAD-7 &lt; 15 and C-SSRS ideation negative and no positive SUD-acuity criterion above</td></tr></tbody></table><p><strong>Immediate escalation (988) triggers</strong></p><ul><li>Any positive C-SSRS high-risk item (ideation 4/5 or any behavior).</li><li>PHQ-9 item 9 positive.</li><li>Patient free-text indicating imminent self-harm.</li></ul><p><strong>Urgent medical (non-988) triggers:</strong> SOWS ≥ 21 (severe withdrawal) routes to urgent medical/clinician review for withdrawal management. On any immediate-escalation trigger, the BHUC front-door / screening flow surfaces the 988 interstitial and the crisis pathway. Raw high-risk scores are clinician-facing and are not shown to the patient.</p>


## Instrument selection and administration order

<p><strong>Which instruments run, and in what order (SBIRT adaptive model)</strong></p><p>Every patient completes the core spine; the SUD battery branches off the NIDA Quick Screen and off AUDIT/DAST-10 positives, so low-risk patients answer few questionnaires.</p><ol><li><strong>C-SSRS first</strong> whenever the visit type requires suicide-risk screening; it must be completed before PHQ-9/GAD-7 unlock.</li><li><strong>PHQ-9</strong> (depression), then <strong>GAD-7</strong> (anxiety).</li><li><strong>NIDA Quick Screen</strong> (SUD gateway) for every patient.</li><li>If NIDA alcohol positive → <strong>AUDIT</strong>. If NIDA drug positive → <strong>DAST-10</strong>.</li><li>If AUDIT ≥ 8 → <strong>Craving &amp; Triggers</strong>. If DAST-10 ≥ 3 → <strong>SOWS</strong>.</li><li>If AUDIT ≥ 8 or DAST-10 ≥ 3 → <strong>BAM</strong> and <strong>SOCRATES</strong> (SOCRATES uses drug wording when the drug path fired, else alcohol wording).</li></ol><p>Instruments are administered one at a time and are never mixed on one screen. C-SSRS uses branching. Scoring is performed server-side by the BHUC Risk Identification Agent; no free-text scoring inputs are accepted client-side. All SUD-battery instruments (NIDA, AUDIT, DAST-10, Craving &amp; Triggers, SOWS, BAM, SOCRATES) collect 42 CFR Part 2 data and their screening rows are labeled <code>part2_sud</code>.</p>
