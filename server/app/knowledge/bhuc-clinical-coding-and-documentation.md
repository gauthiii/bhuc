## BHUC clinical note template (urgent behavioral encounter)

<p><strong>Standard note structure</strong></p><ol><li><strong>Chief complaint</strong> — patient's stated reason for the visit.</li><li><strong>History of present illness (HPI)</strong> — onset, duration, severity, context, prior treatment.</li><li><strong>Screening results</strong> — instrument scores and risk band (clinician-facing).</li><li><strong>Mental status exam (MSE)</strong> — appearance, mood/affect, thought process/content, cognition, insight/judgment, safety.</li><li><strong>Assessment</strong> — diagnostic impression with ICD-10 code(s).</li><li><strong>Plan</strong> — interventions, medications, safety plan, disposition, follow-up, referrals.</li></ol><p>The BHUC Clinical Documentation Agent drafts this note; a human clinician must sign it. Nothing enters the record until signed.</p>


## Documentation integrity and grounding rules

<p><strong>How the agent keeps notes trustworthy</strong></p><ul><li>Every drafted line is tagged <strong>grounded</strong> (supported by the source data) or <strong>unverified</strong>.</li><li>The agent never fabricates clinical detail, vitals, history, or codes.</li><li>Coding suggestions are proposals with a confidence score; the clinician adds or dismisses each.</li><li>At least one ICD-10 diagnosis is required before the note can be signed.</li><li>The agent never signs — a human clinician signs; risk must be confirmed first.</li><li>When the source is structured screening data, a claim is grounded only if it is supported by a screening result in the summary (an instrument name, score, band, flag, or subscale). Interpretive/exam lines not derivable from screening data are tagged unverified for the clinician to confirm.</li></ul>


## Coding and documentation for SUD (42 CFR Part 2)

<p><strong>Handling substance-use content</strong></p><p>Substance-use diagnoses (ICD-10 F10–F19) and any SUD treatment detail are protected under 42 CFR Part 2. In the BHUC UI these fields render masked unless the patient's consent and the clinician's role permit disclosure (enforced server-side by the Part 2 access labels set by the BHUC Consent &amp; Data Protection Agent).</p><p>When drafting notes that touch SUD, the documentation agent honors the Part 2 label: protected specifics are access-gated, and downstream exports (e.g., prior-auth packets) redact protected details unless a valid Part 2 consent is on file. Any note line derived from a SUD-battery instrument (NIDA Quick Screen, AUDIT, DAST-10, Craving &amp; Triggers, SOWS, BAM, SOCRATES) is SUD content and must be treated as Part 2.</p>

## Synthesizing multiple instruments into one assessment

<p><strong>How to combine several screening results into a single coherent note.</strong></p><ul><li><strong>Chief complaint / safety first.</strong> If C-SSRS is high-risk or PHQ-9 item 9 is positive, suicidality leads the Chief Complaint and Plan (crisis pathway). Otherwise lead with the highest-acuity domain.</li><li><strong>Primary vs. secondary diagnosis.</strong> The instrument with the highest severity/risk band anchors the primary diagnosis; other elevated instruments become secondary/comorbid diagnoses (e.g., moderate GAD-7 alongside severe PHQ-9 → primary MDD, secondary GAD).</li><li><strong>Comorbidity.</strong> Document co-occurring mental-health and substance-use findings together; note interactions (e.g., alcohol use worsening depressive symptoms).</li><li><strong>Risk stratification.</strong> The overall risk level is the highest band across instruments; SOWS ≥21 additionally flags an urgent medical (withdrawal-management) need, distinct from a 988 suicide-crisis trigger.</li><li><strong>Readiness → Plan.</strong> Use SOCRATES (recognition/ambivalence/taking-steps) to shape engagement: high Taking Steps → reinforce/continue; high Ambivalence → motivational interviewing; low Recognition → psychoeducation.</li><li><strong>One instrument per domain.</strong> Use only the latest screening per instrument; do not double-count repeated administrations of the same instrument.</li></ul>


## Drafting a note from structured screening results

<p><strong>Note template variant when the source is a structured screening summary (not an ambient transcript).</strong> Every line must trace to a screening result in the summary; interpretive or exam content that the screening data does not support is tagged unverified.</p><ol><li><strong>Chief complaint</strong> — one line derived from the highest-acuity finding (grounded).</li><li><strong>HPI</strong> — one line per elevated domain, each citing the instrument and score: mood (PHQ-9), anxiety (GAD-7), suicidality (C-SSRS), substance-use pattern (NIDA/AUDIT/DAST-10), craving/withdrawal/readiness (Craving/SOWS/SOCRATES). Lines about onset/duration/context not present in the screening data are unverified.</li><li><strong>Screening results</strong> — list each instrument with score, severity, and risk band verbatim from the summary (grounded).</li><li><strong>MSE</strong> — screening is not an exam; MSE lines are unverified unless a screening result directly implies them (e.g., C-SSRS positive → documented risk).</li><li><strong>Assessment</strong> — primary and secondary diagnoses per the interpretation-mapping and synthesis articles, each with the instrument/score as supporting text.</li><li><strong>Plan</strong> — driven by risk band + escalation flags (crisis pathway for C-SSRS/PHQ-9 item 9; urgent medical for SOWS ≥21) + SOCRATES readiness for treatment engagement.</li></ol><p>SUD-battery-derived lines are 42 CFR Part 2 content. The agent never signs — the clinician reviews, resolves unverified lines, and signs.</p>
